  • Lensing Modeling
Lensing modeling is a technique to reconstruct the mass distribution of lenses. It is a critical processing for converting lensing signals to usable constrains for astrophysics and cosmology. Nowadays, there are several tools for lensing modeling, but a criterion for choosing a proper tool for a specific observation or lensing system is still not clear. Our goal is to find this criterion using our simulations. As we can produce identical simulated lensed images to eyes of human for different telescopes. One interesting application is 3 to build a standard simulated database for lensing modelers. They can model the mass distribution of the lens without knowing the correct input lens. By comparing the models and input mass distribution, lensing modelers can figure out the advantages and disadvantages of their methods. Simulations for different types of lensing systems and different telescopes are involved in this project. We are planning to start with cluster scale lensing systems from HST and Magellan Telescope.
