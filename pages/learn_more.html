Strong Lensing

Strong gravitational lensing happens when the deflection angle is large enough to produce multiple images, arcs, or even Einstein rings. Fig. 2 shows typical strong lensed observations, it includes, for point-like background sources, there will be multiple images (up-right panel); for extended background emissions, there can be arcs (up-middle panel) or rings (up-left panel).
